

# Problem Context

| Segment        | Typical Context                                                                                  | Core Frictions                                                                                                                              |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Casual players | Use a chess board at home or find a Meetup group.                                                | Many often lose their chess board or do not have anyone interested in playing chess near them                                               |
| Organizers     | Host a physical venue and provide all required equipment. Appoint arbiter and recruit volunteers | Offline club competitions often require significant logistical commitment, such as travel time and parking, which can deter casual players. |

# Current State (As-Is)

* It is hard to find chess players near you as a casual person.
* Offline games cannot be recorded conveniently and replayed.
* The commitment and logistics of hosting chess competitions offline is more expensive.
* The distance between players results in extra time wasted on travel.

# Business Impact

| Impact Area                  | Current Cost                                                                                                 | Expected Benefit                                                                                         |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Player Matching & Game Setup | High time and effort spent finding opponents manually; dependency on physical presence or external platforms | Instant matchmaking with global availability, reducing wait time and increasing game frequency           |
| Platform Infrastructure      | Fragmented tools (chat apps, social media, offline coordination) increase coordination cost                  | Unified system for accounts, matchmaking, and gameplay, improving efficiency and user retention          |
| Community Engagement         | Limited engagement due to lack of centralized platform and tracking system                                   | Active community with friends list, leaderboards, and game history, increasing long-term user engagement |
| Accessibility                | Physical location constraints and lack of nearby players reduce opportunities                                | 24/7 access to chess matches from anywhere, enabling consistent practice and competition                 |
| Game Management              | No automated tracking of matches, results, or player progress                                                | Automated game history, replays, and statistics for performance improvement and analysis                 |

# Problem Statement

Chess players often struggle to find available opponents for regular practice and competitive play.Current matchmaking methods are mostly manual, slow, and depend on external communication.There is no simple centralized platform for real-time play, game tracking, and user interaction.This project aims to develop an online chess platform to improve accessibility, matchmaking, and engagement.
